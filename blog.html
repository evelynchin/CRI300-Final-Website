Podcasts We

Posted on Nov 1 2015

With so many shows, hosts, and topics covered, we believe there's a podcast out there for everyone. Here are some under 20 minute podcast episodes that are perfect for a short coffee break. Learn about interesting topics all while enjoying your favourite cup of joe

"Vexillionaire" from 99% Percent Invisible

Flags are great and so is Roman Mars, host of 99% Invisible. "Vexillionaire" is all about flag design. Follow along with the accompanying show notes to see the flags mentioned.

Want more? Roman Mars also did a great Ted Talk on flag design at #TED2015!

Perfect for those who like: design, flags and cities.


"This Proves Everything" from Reply All

Hosted by PJ Vogt and Alex Goldman, Reply All is a show about the internet. "This Proves Everything" covers a crazy love story conspiracy theory surrounding one of the world's biggest boy bands.

Perfect for those who like: crazy internet theories, love stories or boy bands.

"Jeff Beal (House of Cards)" from Song Exploder

Song Exploder is a podcast where musicians take apart their songs and is hosted by Hrishikesh Hirway. This episode invited Jeff Beal, the composer of the House of Card main theme, to deconstruct the track and how the theme evolves as the show progresses onto the second season.

Perfect for those who like: music, listening to someone's thought process or House of Cards.

"A City On The Moon" from Planet Money

Planning your next vacation? Ever thought about visiting the frozen city of Reykjavik, Iceland? Learn about the city's history and how it began its prosperous economy in this episode of NPR's Planet Money.

Perfect for those who like: geography, traveling or winter.


Top 5 Coffee Cities

Posted on Nov 3 2015

Thinking of where to take your next trip? Why not stop by one of our top 5 coffee cities? One of the great things about coffee is how diverse it can be and how it differs in different parts of the world. Stay well caffeinated while exploring these awesome cities!



Rome, Italy
Rome's history spans more than two and a half thousand years so it's no question as to why it's called "The Eternal City". Stop off a day in Rome by stoping in one of its countless coffe bars and ordering an Italian espresso. Not sure how to order in Italy? Don't worry, we got you!

Where to visit: Grab a cuppa coffee from Sant Eustachio Il Caffe, an authentic Italian cafe that has been around since 1938.



Seattle, USA
Seattle, Washington is the coffee captial of America! No other city consumes as much coffee as Seattleites. This West Coast city has become known for its coffee culture and is home to numerous roasters and coffeehouses.

Where to visit: Espresso Vivace in Capitol Hill. Also look out for the original Starbucks in Pike Place Market!



Melbourne, Australia
Let's head down under to Melbourne! Located on the southern coast of Australia, Melbourne may be home to the best cafes in the world.

Where to visit: Patricia on the corner of Little Bourke and Little William.



Vienna, Austria
Full of history, culture and attractions, Vienna is another one of our top coffee cities. In October 2011, "Viennese Coffee House Culture" was listed as "Intangible Cultural Heritage" in Austria by UNESCO. Stop by a Viennese cafe and enjoy its unqiue and cozy atmosphere.

Where to visit: Enjoy the breathtaking arched ceilings of Cafe Central and grab a Viennese breakfast with your coffee!



Taipei, Taiwan
Home to 2.7 million people, Taipei is the political, economic, educational and cultural center of Taiwan. The streets are full of cafes and coffee shops and Taipei is quickly becoming the world's next great coffee city!

Where to visit: Dating back to 1956, Fong Da, is one of Taipai's original coffee shops. Try one of their signature iced coffees!
